Initial
Stakeholder Mapping

A structured mapping of everyone connected to the AIUK Community Platform project — categorised by their level of interest and involvement, to guide how the team communicates and collaborates.

About this document

This document outlines the results of a stakeholder mapping activity carried out at the start of the AIUK Community Platform project. Stakeholders are categorised into four quadrants based on their interest and involvement levels.

The mapping is a living reference — it informed how the project team communicated progress, sought input, and structured participation throughout the project. A more detailed Stakeholder Mapping + Glossary was produced later in Phase One.

Manage Closely

Stakeholders with both high interest and high involvement. These people are working directly with the project team and the aim is to encourage active collaboration throughout.

High interest · High involvement

Manage Closely

Paid Staff
  • Community Organisers — actively involved in shaping engagement strategies and mobilising activists through the platform
  • Community Platform Project Group — integral to decision-making, design, and implementation
  • Education Officers — responsible for organising educational groups and potentially resources on the platform
  • Community Manager — plays a central role in managing the platform's community, engagement strategies, and safeguarding measures
  • Director of Activism and Education (project sponsor) — oversees the strategic direction of activism and education initiatives, ensuring the platform supports Amnesty's broader organisational strategy
  • Activist and Education Department Managers — likely involved in integrating educational content and activism tools into the platform
  • Activist events organisers — may use the platform to coordinate logistics for events
Volunteers
  • Right holders — require direct involvement to ensure their needs are central to the platform's development
  • Lead Activists — likely to provide feedback and actively use the platform to mobilise grassroots campaigns. There are 360 Lead Activists who have Amnesty email addresses and access to training on diversity, GDPR, and safeguarding
  • Building a Powerful Movement (board committee) — monitors the effectiveness of activist initiatives on the platform, offering strategic advice
  • Country coordinators — specialise in human rights campaigning for specific countries and support local activism efforts
  • Activism Reps — provide regional support to activists, ensuring their engagement with national campaigns through the platform
Networks
  • Feminist Network — likely to collaborate closely to ensure gender equality is prioritised in the platform's design and operations
Agencies
  • WAO — core team (together with AIUK) delivering this work

Keep Satisfied

Stakeholders with high interest but lower direct involvement. The team keeps them satisfied with regular updates and seeks their input at key moments.

High interest · Lower involvement

Keep Satisfied

Paid Staff
  • Fundraising Team — may require regular updates on how the platform supports fundraising campaigns
  • Campaigns Team — could need insights on how the platform aligns with broader campaign goals
  • AIUK Transformation Oversight Committee — likely to expect periodic reports on progress and alignment with organisational objectives
  • Data, Digital and Tech Transformation team (other than Elly and Andrea) — kept informed of project progress and any implications for other transformation projects, e.g. CRM integration
  • Digital Engagement Team — this is a digital product and while not directly managed by this team, important for alignment across Digital and Activism and Education teams
  • Supporter Communications Team — currently deal with many activist queries; the platform could impact future ways of working
Volunteers
  • Trade Union network — may expect occasional updates on how their interests are reflected
  • Teacher Advisory Group — could provide feedback on functions or features with educators in mind
  • Network Committees — likely to provide strategic input on functions the platform requires
  • Nations (staff and activists) — could require updates on how the platform supports regional human rights initiatives
  • Activist Events committee — may help ensure the platform facilitates effective planning and promotion of activist events across the UK
Networks
  • Coalition and partners — likely to expect regular communication about collaborative opportunities
  • Anti-Racism Network — might provide input on ensuring anti-racist practices are embedded in the platform design
Other
  • AIUK's Digital Action Takers — might see the platform as a next step in their engagement with AIUK
  • Online Course Takers (first touch engagement) — might see the platform as a next step in their engagement with AIUK

Keep Informed

Stakeholders with lower interest but higher direct involvement. They should be kept informed about developments to ensure alignment.

Lower interest · Higher involvement

Keep Informed

Volunteers
  • Youth Collective — likely to use the platform for youth-focused campaigns and updates on activism opportunities
  • Activist Led Campaigns — could require tools to manage their campaigns independently while staying informed about broader AIUK activities
  • Working Party / Resolution Proposers — might require broadcasting tools to help community work in solidarity with policy initiatives
  • Students and youth networks (schools, FE, and universities)
Networks
  • Local Groups — may use the platform to coordinate local human rights initiatives and events
  • Words that Burn (Creatives for Human Rights) — likely to engage with creative resources and programmes shared via the platform
  • Student Action Network — represents the interests of university student groups
  • Children's Human Rights Network — likely to use the platform to coordinate campaigns on children's rights
Agencies
  • Torchbox — support and guidance for ensuring a seamless user experience between the website, knowledge hub, and community platforms

Monitor

Stakeholders with lower levels of both involvement and interest who should still be monitored for potential engagement or impact.

Lower interest · Lower involvement

Monitor

Paid Staff
  • Amnesty International Secretariat (AIIS staff involved in their community platform) — may provide occasional feedback or insights but are not actively involved
  • Comms team — could monitor communication trends and ensure messaging consistency across platforms
Volunteers
  • Rise Up participants and alumni — likely to engage sporadically
  • Teachers / Educators — may occasionally use the platform
  • Football Welcomes / Women's Leadership Programme — possible user with focus on inclusivity initiatives for refugee women and girls
  • Disabled People's Human Rights network — could contribute perspectives on accessibility needs
  • Youth Coaches — might use the platform sporadically for training resources and youth engagement tools
Networks
  • Anti-Death Penalty Project (network) — might organise via the platform or share campaign updates
  • Rainbow Network — may offer occasional updates on LGBTQ+ inclusivity efforts within the platform

Next steps from this mapping

This initial mapping informed the design of the project's communication and engagement strategy. A more detailed Stakeholder Mapping + Glossary document was produced later in Phase One, expanding definitions and refining categories as the team's understanding of the stakeholder landscape deepened.

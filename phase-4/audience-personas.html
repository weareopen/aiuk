Audience Refinement &
Persona Spectrums

A refined audience approach and persona spectrums for the AIUK Community Platform — defining who we are building for, and the spectrum of needs from new advocates to seasoned community leaders.

About this document

Building power and making change is a collective endeavour. The AIUK Community Platform is not being built for everybody — it is being built for those who choose to step inside. Social media is the window, but activists are welcome to come inside. Activism is more like a café than a secret conversation.

This document draws on user research from the AIUK Data Report and proposes a refined audience approach, along with persona spectrums that highlight potential user groups and their distinct needs.

This document is held in Google Docs and is accessible to project team members.

The potential audience

The AIUK audience is wide, diverse and multi-faceted. Stakeholder mapping shows a variety of connection points, audience groupings and different ways to think about the AIUK audience as a whole. An interactive stakeholder map helps illustrate the full landscape.

Insights from an April 2024 DaAT presentation informed thinking about how active different audience groups might be on the community platform. Groups likely to engage with the digital platform were mapped alongside stakeholder quadrants — those to "Engage Closely" and "Keep Satisfied." Audience groups with an engagement rating of three or higher were prioritised.

🌐

Digital Action Takers

People who engage primarily online — signing petitions, sharing content, participating in digital campaigns.

🤝

Active Volunteers

Regular participants in AIUK activities — attending events, joining local groups, taking part in campaigns.

🏛️

Community Leaders

Organisers who run groups and networks, coordinate country responses, and mentor other activists.

Refined audience focus

The potential audience for the AIUK Community Platform is huge and complex. To build with intention and serve the needs of those activists most likely to not only engage but also promote, improve, and help populate the platform, the project started with a focus on Community Leaders of Amnesty groups and networks.

These are the educators and activists who sit on committees, coordinate country responses, build youth or teacher networks, run local groups, and organise communities. This group includes a variety of both volunteers and staff — and they are engaged and responsive.

Why start with Community Leaders?

Focusing on the existing activist base — the people already engaged with AIUK as activists and educators — before trying to build for wider conversion ensures the platform is well-grounded. This audience is extremely diverse in demographics and digital acumen, so focusing on known community activists will be representative of the wider potential audience.

As the platform launches, the focus should be on Organiser activists while also inviting in all members of the various groups. These are the first community members — and understanding what they have in common requires assembling Persona Spectrums.

Persona spectrums

A Persona Spectrum is an approach that groups people by shared motivations or constraints. Rather than a single story, it is a collection of related experiences which highlight how a particular need can be met in different circumstances.

This approach considers a wider set of needs. For example, voice recognition software benefits those with a permanent speech impairment, someone who has lost their voice temporarily, or a person whose hands are occupied. By focusing on motivations and constraints, persona spectrums encourage solutions useful to more people — not just a narrowly defined type of community member.

In the AIUK context, there are both activists and educators. The needs of these two groups overlap significantly, but each also has distinct needs explored below.

Activist spectrum

Although initial focus is on Community Leaders, the goal is to build an AIUK community that is sustainable and accessible for all activists — new or experienced. The three-step activist persona spectrum is:

🌱

New Human Rights Advocates

Newcomers, casual users, digital action takers, curious people exploring human rights issues for the first time.

✊

Active Volunteers

Regular users, community members, activists, educators, volunteers engaged in ongoing campaign and community work.

🏆

AIUK Community Leaders

Committee members, country coordinators, local group leaders, long-term activists who organise and mentor others.

Educator spectrum

A second spectrum focuses specifically on educators to capture the unique or outlying needs of teachers, trainers, and people who discuss human rights in classrooms or workshops:

📖

Novice Educator

A teacher or trainer new to human rights education but eager to learn. May or may not have existing community relationships.

🎓

Educational Practitioner

Actively using AIUK programmes and resources, with some connections to other community members.

⭐

Veteran Educator

Highly experienced in human rights organising and education, able to mentor and support other AIUK educators in various ways.

Features useful across the spectrum

Using research-backed Community Platform User Stories, five themes and associated user stories across the spectrum were established to identify base-level feature requirements. A full list is available in the User Stories — Community Persona Spectrums spreadsheet.

Communication & Interaction

  • Direct Messaging – Private messaging system with appropriate privacy controls
  • Email Digests – Automatic email digests and on-demand group messaging by email
  • Inclusive Messaging – Inclusive language guidelines and alternative terminology suggestions
  • Local Connection – Geographic filtering and local group organisation tools
  • Notifications – Customisable notifications, digests and alerts
  • Search – Comprehensive search functionality with filters and suggestions

User Management & Identity

  • Onboarding workflow – Automated workflow for onboarding, including guidance and user trust levels
  • Permissions – Content and discussion topic creation/sharing permissions
  • Profile – Customisable user profiles
  • Trust Levels – Customisable member trust levels

Safety & Compliance

  • Content Warnings – The ability to hide content behind a warning message
  • GDPR & Legal – GDPR compliance
  • Moderation – Moderation tools available to appropriate user levels
  • Privacy – Granular privacy controls for profiles, groups, and discussion threads

Recognition & Motivation

  • Engagement – Recognition of different types and levels of activism and trust levels
  • Recognition – Open Recognition in the form of digital badges and credentials
  • Impact – Impact tracking and reporting dashboard with multiple detail levels
  • Variety – Visibility of types of activism opportunities available
  • Templates – Posting guides and templates

Accessibility & Usability

  • Accessibility – Adherence to accessibility guidelines
  • Bookmarking – Bookmarking and favouriting functionality
  • Languages – Interface available in multiple languages
  • Pinning Content – Functionality to pin content so all users see it
  • Purpose – Pop-up/banner for users who aren't logged in or are new, giving purpose and links to Code of Conduct

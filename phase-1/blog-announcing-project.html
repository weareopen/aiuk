Announcing an Amnesty Community Project

The opening post introducing the Community Platform Project — what it is, why it matters, and how AIUK activists can get involved from the very start.

Introduction

We know that some of our content and information is hard to find and can feel outdated, and we know that it is hard to see into our vibrant, grassroots community. We want to make it easier for people to connect and work together towards our common goals.

We need better ways to build a powerful movement together.

The Community Platform Project is going to help us get there. Over the next months, we will be asking for your engagement. We want to ensure that what we do truly helps you, individual community members, organise and campaign together with Amnesty International UK.

Our partners

We're not alone! Amnesty International UK is working with We Are Open Co-op and Torchbox, two worker-owned organisations that specialise in digital transformation and community building.

🌐

New Website

A rebuilt public-facing website to make AIUK's content more accessible and up to date for all visitors.

📚

Knowledge Hub

A dedicated space for activist resources, guides, and shared knowledge — easier to find and use.

🤝

Community Platform

The heart of this project — an online space to help activists connect, collaborate, and campaign together.

We are working to replace old systems and build a new website, a knowledge hub, and this community platform. We are working to make sure that these three projects inform one another and connect together.

Sharing openly

We want to share openly, via this blog, about how it's going, and are always happy for your feedback. Please leave comments on our posts and let us know what your thoughts are.

Why openness matters

Sharing the process as we go — not just the outcomes — means the community can shape this project in real time. Every post in this blog is an invitation to engage, question, and contribute.

Initial ways to get involved

There are three immediate ways to be part of this project from the beginning:

  • Check out our Project Charter — understand the scope, principles, and goals of the project
  • Fill in our initial survey — share your needs and experiences to help shape what we build
  • Keep an eye on this blog — follow along as we document progress, share decisions, and ask for input

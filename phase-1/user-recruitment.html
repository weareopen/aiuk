User Recruitment
Plan

Recruiting users to inform community strategy design and platform selection for the AIUK Community Platform project — covering target audiences, channels, materials, incentives, and diversity considerations.

⚠ Deprecated Document

This document has been superseded. Please refer to the User Research and Platform Testing Guide for the current version of this guidance.

Overview

Three user groups have been identified for the AIUK community platform. Focusing on the primary and secondary users of the platform, a survey will be sent to identify broad needs and suitability for inviting participants to either a focus group or an individual interview. The aim is to gather enough information to create a realistic needs analysis based on the diverse needs of the community.

The needs analysis, alongside desk-based research, will inform the community platforms comparison. This data will be analysed collaboratively to reach a decision on which platforms to test. Ideally, AIUK stakeholders will test three platforms — with a minimum of two — so that they can compare and contrast options. A further survey will allow the team to gather a range of data, from user sentiment to red flags and thoughts about functionality. Focus groups may then be run, if required, to help make a final decision on choosing a community platform for AIUK.

Target Audiences

Drawing from the Stakeholder Mapping and Glossary, these audiences are likely to use or manage the platform. Users recruited for user research do not map one-to-one with stakeholders — many stakeholders are involved or interested in the project but will not be actively using the platform.

Tier 1 — Primary Users / Core Advocates

The most engaged and influential users who are actively driving forward community development and platform use. This includes activists currently engaged with AIUK and staff working directly in the platform.

  • Paid Staff:
    • Community Organiser (×2)
    • Education Officer
    • Community Manager
    • Activist Events Organisers
  • Volunteers:
    • Lead Activists (from networks in focus groups)
    • Country Coordinators
    • Activism Reps
    • Building a Powerful Movement (board committee members)
    • Activist Events committee

Tier 2 — Secondary Users / Engaged Contributors

These people participate regularly but may not have the same level of influence or commitment as Core Advocates. They are engaged activists or staff who may use the platform and/or participate in community policy development and processes.

  • Paid Staff:
    • Activist and Education Department Managers
    • Digital Engagement Team
    • Supporters Comms Team
  • Volunteers:
    • Networks and Network Committees
    • Teacher Advisory Group
    • Youth Collective
    • Local Groups

Tier 3 — Tertiary Users / Potential Activators

This group should be considered separately. Important stakeholders should be identified for individual interviews, then snowball sampling used to broaden the pool. Cutting across this tier are HRE's current audiences.

  • Paid Staff:
    • Fundraising Teams
    • Campaigns Team
  • Volunteers / HRE Audiences:
    • Activism education — e.g. members of the public enrolled on courses (FutureLearn/Academy), activists participating in trainings, Youth Coach volunteers, professionals working on ESCR rights
    • Creative education — e.g. teachers being trained through partnerships, poets, partners for Words That Burn
    • Formal education — e.g. teachers on year-long CPD course, alumni of that course, mentee teachers, Teacher Advisory Group, coalition partners

Recruitment Channels

  • AIUK's existing database (newsletters, mailings, direct email)
  • Snowball sampling with Community Organisers and Lead Activists
  • Engaged representatives from groups and committees (staff advisement)

Recruitment Materials

Respondents should be pre-qualified through screener questions. Whether reaching out via email, social media, or other formats, potential interviewees should be directed to an online form. This form should also obtain their consent for data processing.

  • Survey (including consent form, information about the project, and consent for further discussion)
  • Media and/or newsletter assets (to share the survey)
  • Interview invitation
  • Follow-up email templates

Incentive Structure

The question of whether a budget is available to remunerate participants for their time should be confirmed at the outset. If not, an alternative means of recognising contributions should be identified — for example, acknowledgement via newsletters — and participants may be offered early access to the community platform as a gesture of appreciation.

Diversity and Inclusion

The following considerations should inform all recruitment activity:

  • Ensure representation across geographic regions
  • Include activists with varying levels of experience
  • Consider accessibility needs for participation
  • Recruit across different types of human rights work
  • Ensure language diversity with translation support as needed

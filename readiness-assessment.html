Post-pilot Readiness Assessment Framework

The criteria and data sources used to determine whether the Amnesty Community pilot demonstrated readiness to scale — including a Go/No-Go decision matrix and survey structure.

About this document

This framework sets out the criteria for deciding whether the Amnesty Community pilot demonstrated sufficient readiness to proceed to a general launch. It covers pre-pilot testing requirements, the data sources used to collect evidence during the pilot, a structured Go/No-Go decision matrix, and examples of each decision outcome.

Note: The pilot was based on users aged 18+. The question of whether the platform is ready for users under that age is not addressed in this document and is covered separately.

This document is held in Google Docs and is accessible to project team members.

Pre-pilot testing

Before the pilot commenced, two tests were required to pass:

Platform testing with users

Five users performed a series of tasks and completed the System Usability Scale (SUS). This is outlined in the Platform Testing Plan (January 2026). The platform had to pass this test before the pilot could proceed.

Basic accessibility testing

Freely available tools were used to test the platform against the accessibility guidelines established at project inception. Both tests were required to pass before the pilot could begin.

Go/No-Go Decision Matrix

The matrix below provides a holistic approach to the launch decision. It covers four domains — Usability, Engagement, Safety, and Technical — each with defined success criteria and the data sources used to measure them.

Domain Success Criteria Data Sources
Usability
  • SUS score ≥ 68
  • 75% task completion rate
  • Mobile experience ≥ 4/5 rating
 SUS survey; Feedback survey
Engagement
  • DAU/MAU ≥ 15%
  • 50% of topics have at least one reply
  • Average response time ≤ 8 hours
 Admin dashboard reports
Safety
  • Flag resolution within ≤ 72 hours
  • Safety rating > 4/5
  • Zero unresolved incidents
 Admin dashboard reports; Feedback survey
Technical
  • Zero WCAG AA critical issues
  • 99.5% platform uptime
  • Page speed ≥ 4/5
 Accessibility audit; Uptime log + survey

Data sources

Discourse Admin Dashboard

Built-in reports accessible at /admin/dashboard/reports provided the following metrics during the pilot:

  • Daily Engaged Users — percentage of active users (target: ≥ 15% during pilot)
  • New Contributors — percentage posting for the first time (target: ≥ 40% of pilot users)
  • Time to First Response — average hours before a reply (target: ≤ 8 hours)
  • Topics with No Response — unresponded questions (target: ≤ 20%)
  • Flags Status — moderation response time (target: ≤ 24 hours average)
  • Suspicious Logins — security incidents (target: zero unresolved)
  • DAU/MAU Ratio — daily active users ÷ monthly active users (target: ≥ 15%)

External surveys

Surveys were distributed via Discourse announcements at key points during the pilot:

End of Week 1

Quick Check (3 questions, ~1 min)

Overall satisfaction · Ease of use · Mobile experience quality (all 1–5 scale)

End of Week 2

Structured Feedback (7 questions, ~5 mins)

Task completion · Features used · Safety perception · Navigation clarity · Open text · NPS

End of Pilot Cohort

SUS Survey (10 questions, ~3 mins)

Standardised usability questionnaire administered at end of each pilot phase. Target score ≥ 68.

End of Pilot

Full Survey (10–15 mins)

Overall sentiment · Platform usefulness by purpose · Net Promoter Score · Branching logic

Decision outputs

The framework defines three possible outcomes from the post-pilot review, each with different implications for next steps:

✅ Good to Go!

Based on pilot data, the platform meets or exceeds all success criteria. Recommend general launch with initial intensive monitoring and quarterly health reviews.

👍 Conditional Go

Platform exceeds engagement and safety criteria but SUS score falls slightly below target (e.g. 66 rather than 68) due to specific navigation issues. Recommend launch with immediate UX improvements planned, and re-assessment at 30 days.

❌ No-Go

Safety metrics unmet — for example, average flag resolution time 48 hours against a 24-hour target, or moderation tooling insufficient for activist community scale. Recommend deferring launch, expanding moderation capacity, and re-piloting in 6 weeks.

Important scope note

This framework covers readiness for a general launch with adult users (18+). A separate process applies to the question of extending access to users under 18. See the Online Safety Act advice and guidance for the child safety compliance framework.

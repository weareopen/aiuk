Project
Management Notes

A running record of decisions, stand-ups, and retrospectives across the full life of the project — the backup and companion to the primary Trello-based project management board.

About this document

The Project Management Notes document is a longitudinal record of the AIUK Community Platform project — capturing decisions made, actions agreed, blockers surfaced, and progress noted across the full eighteen months. It was listed in the project index as the "back up AIUK Community Platform PM doc" and exists alongside the primary project management board in Trello.

Where Trello captures individual tasks and their associated comments, this document captures the narrative thread — the why behind decisions, the texture of discussions, and the evolving understanding of the project as it moved through its phases.

Why this document exists

Trello is excellent for task management but less good as a narrative record. This document serves as an accessible, searchable companion — useful for onboarding new team members, reflecting at retrospectives, and maintaining an honest account of how the project developed.

How the project was managed

The project ran on two-week heartbeats — a rhythm described in the Project Charter. At the start of each heartbeat the team assessed upcoming work; at the end there was a short retrospective. The notes document captures the outputs of both.

The heartbeat rhythm

Each two-week cycle began with a lightweight planning check-in and ended with a brief "what went well / what could be better" retro. This kept the project responsive without introducing the overhead of formal sprint ceremonies.

Stand-ups between WAO and AIUK team members happened asynchronously in text, with fortnightly video calls to make decisions and clear blockers. Notes from those calls were captured here.

Stand-up format

Text-based stand-ups followed a simple three-part format, allowing team members to update asynchronously across different schedules and time zones.

1

What I did

A brief note on progress since the last stand-up — tasks completed, documents drafted, meetings held.

2

What I'm working on

Current focus for the heartbeat — what's in progress and what's being planned.

3

Any blockers

Anything that needs a decision, a conversation, or help from another team member to move forward.

What the notes contain

Across the life of the project, the notes document captures:

Types of content across the document

  • Bi-weekly check-in notes — summaries of WAO/AIUK video calls, including decisions made and next actions agreed
  • Stand-up entries — asynchronous text updates from team members across the heartbeat
  • Retrospective outputs — what worked, what didn't, what to change in the next heartbeat
  • Decision log — key decisions and the reasoning behind them, particularly where the team chose between options
  • Risk notes — emerging risks surfaced during meetings, alongside the actions taken to address them
  • Handover information — notes useful for any new team members joining mid-project

The document also includes links to key Trello cards, Google Docs, and other resources — making it a useful entry point for navigating the full project archive.

Relationship to the Trello board

The primary project management tool was Trello, chosen because it keeps tasks and comments together in a transparent, living record that anyone can check at any time. The Project Management Notes document is the narrative complement — the Trello board shows what was being worked on; this document records how the team was thinking about it.

Access and permissions

The Trello board is internal to AIUK staff and the project team. The Project Management Notes document is accessible to project team members. Both are referenced in the project's Charter as the primary mechanisms for tracking progress and decisions.

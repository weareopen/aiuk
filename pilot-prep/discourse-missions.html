Discourse Missions

A structured sequence of guided activities designed to help pilot participants explore the Amnesty Community platform — organised by phase, group, and mission drop.

About this document

This document sets out the Discourse Missions used during the AIUK Community Platform Pilot. Missions are structured activities that guided participants through key features of the platform, grouped into four "Mission Drops" and delivered across different pilot phases to community leaders, general network members, and new activists.

Each mission drop was released to groups at staggered intervals, giving the team time to process feedback and make improvements. Participants were also encouraged to use the Site Feedback category on the platform for general comments and questions.

This document is held in Google Docs and is accessible to project team members.

Pilot schedule overview

The pilot ran across four phases, each targeting a different group of participants. Mission drops A–I were released in the first week, J–P in the second, Q–V in the third, and W–Z in the fourth. Each phase concluded with a feedback workshop and survey.

Mission Drop #1 — Getting started

The first drop focused on essential onboarding tasks: completing the Welcome Banner, joining relevant networks and groups, making a first post, and personalising one's profile. These missions were designed to be low-stakes and confidence-building.

Drop 1

Missions 1–9: Orientation & Profile Setup

  1. Complete the Welcome Banner — Find and complete the quick-start steps on the home page banner, including introductions and accessing training.
  2. Join a Network or Local Group — Browse available Networks, use the Map feature to find your region, and join groups relevant to your role.
  3. Post your first update or question — Once you've joined a Network or group, make your first post. Suggested prompts are provided.
  4. Experiment with the text editor — Try formatting tools: bold, bullets, quotes, and headings.
  5. Use emojis to react to posts — Click the Heart icon on a post you appreciate to send a notification and build community culture.
  6. Explore your profile — Add a profile picture, location, bio, and adjust notification settings via the profile icon.
  7. Update your profile picture — Upload an image to help other members recognise you.
  8. Fill in your About Me section — Write a short bio including your Amnesty involvement and a fun fact.
  9. Adjust for readability — Explore Interface settings to adjust text size for your screen or device.

Mission Drop #2 — Engaging deeper

The second drop introduced more interactive features: replying with GIFs, starting discussion threads, tagging people, securing accounts with two-factor authentication, and managing notification noise.

Drop 2

Missions 10–15: Interaction & Settings

  1. Post a reply with an animated GIF — Head to the Café Random category and post a GIF reply to test the interface in a low-pressure setting.
  2. Start a discussion thread — Begin a topic relevant to your AIUK work. For bonus points, mention someone using @ and explore categories and the Site Feedback area. Configure your sidebar categories.
  3. Update your profile banner — Upload a banner image to your profile for a personal touch.
  4. Secure your account — Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) via Preferences → Security.
  5. Build your network — Visit a member whose posts you enjoy and click Follow to stay connected.
  6. Mute and Unmute threads — Use the Mute control to hide noisy categories or threads, and unmute them later.

Mission Drop #3 — Advanced features

Mission Drop #3 introduced calendar features, user card customisation, notification preferences, and homepage personalisation — helping participants make the platform work for their individual needs.

Drop 3

Missions 16–21: Personalisation & Discovery

  1. Use your calendar — Locate the Calendar feature in the top menu, view upcoming events, RSVP, or create a test event.
  2. Customise your User Card — Upload a background image for the popup that appears when others click your avatar.
  3. Change your notifications — Visit Preferences → Emails to control digest frequency and keep your inbox manageable.
  4. Review the FAQ — Visit the FAQ section and suggest additions or improvements if you spot gaps.
  5. Experiment with a different Home Page — Set the default home page to Latest, Categories, or Top posts in Preferences.
  6. Customise your menu — Use the pencil icon above categories to rearrange your sidebar navigation.

Mission Drop #4 — Final activities

The final mission drop focused on advanced search, anonymous mode, badges, and — for moderators — issuing pro-social behaviour badges to recognise valued community contributions.

Drop 4

Missions 22–26: Mastery & Recognition

  1. Adjust your notifications — Visit a category that interests you and change to "Watching" or "Tracking" to stay informed.
  2. Try the Advanced Search — Use the magnifying glass to search by date or user. Mastering search prevents duplicate questions.
  3. Experiment with Anonymous Mode — Switch to Anonymous Mode via your user menu to ask sensitive questions without linking them to your public profile. Remember to switch back!
  4. Earn badges for community recognition — Visit the Badges page, pick a badge to earn (e.g. "Editor" or "Nice Reply"), and try to achieve it.
  5. (For moderators) Issue pro-social behaviour badges — Recognise community members with badges including:
    • Social Butterfly — Created an event on a shared calendar
    • Resource Sharer — Amplifying content and engaging others
    • Bridge Builder — Tagging another group or communicating across groups
    • Harmony Keeper — Managing conflict in the community
    • Problem Reporter — Pointing out things that aren't quite right

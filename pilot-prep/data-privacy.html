Data Privacy for
the Community Platform

Guidance for AIUK on implementing minimum viable data collection practices — grounding the platform in human rights-based data protection and exemplifying the highest standards of privacy advocacy.

Summary

This document provides guidance for Amnesty International UK (AIUK) on implementing minimum viable data collection practices for the new online community platform. Given Amnesty's position as a leading human rights organisation and its existing commitment to privacy advocacy, data practices must exemplify the highest standards of human rights-based data protection.

Issues around data privacy and protection were expressed in the project risk register. This document is intended to guide best practices for the community platform.

This document is held in Google Docs and is accessible to project team members.

Relevant context

As a human rights organisation, AIUK has a unique responsibility to protect vulnerable activists and users worldwide, extending beyond the minimum required by regulation.

1. Organisational data protection

AIUK's privacy notice (last updated September 2023) outlines strong data protection principles consistent with GDPR and UK data protection law, including lawful, fair, and transparent processing, data minimisation, storage limitation, security, and rights of individuals. The notice states:

From AIUK's privacy notice

"Generally, we do not collect or keep a record of sensitive information. If we do need to gather this information (for example, to enable you to participate in an athletic event on our behalf) we only do so with your explicit consent. We have measures in place to protect your sensitive information and its confidentiality."

2. Regulatory environment

The UK Online Safety Act (OSA), which came into force on 17 March 2025, means that platforms have a legal duty to protect their users from illegal content online, enforced by Ofcom. OSA requirements for adult platforms include:

  • Implementing effective and proportionate age verification
  • Risk assessments focused on illegal content rather than child harmful content
  • Content moderation, user complaint mechanisms, transparency reporting, and cooperation with Ofcom

3. Effective and proportionate age verification

To meet the OSA age verification requirements while safeguarding user privacy, AIUK should use modern, privacy-first verification technologies such as Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKP), which allow users to cryptographically prove they are over 18 without revealing any personal data or date of birth details.

4. Human rights obligations

As a human rights organisation, AIUK faces unique responsibilities that extend beyond standard GDPR compliance:

  • A duty of care to activists, including human rights defenders who may be at risk and marginalised community members
  • Ensuring data practices align with Amnesty International's advocacy positions on digital rights
  • Serving users in jurisdictions with varying human rights protections

5. Risk register

The risk register for this project outlines the following preventative measures:

  • Data minimisation
  • Guidance and training for activists on digital security best practices
  • Mandatory safeguarding training
  • Clear Code of Conduct
  • Stress testing the platform before going live

Recommendations

The following five recommendations outline a privacy-first and human rights-centred approach for AIUK's online community platform:

Five core recommendations

  • Absolute minimum data collection: Implement a "privacy-first" approach collecting only essential operational data
  • Graduated disclosure model: Allow progressive sharing as trust and engagement develop through the platform and community initiatives
  • Explicit consent frameworks: Go beyond GDPR compliance to demonstrate human rights leadership
  • Digital security by design: Implement technical safeguards that protect activists and vulnerable community members
  • Implement ZKP: Ensure AIUK community members can prove they are adults without sharing personal data

Minimum viable data collection framework

This framework outlines the minimum data AIUK should collect to launch and operate its online community platform securely and responsibly. It prioritises essential operational data, allows optional user-controlled enhancements, and strictly prohibits unnecessary or sensitive data collection.

✅

Tier 1: Essential Operational Data (Mandatory)

Essential data that must be collected to enable core platform functionality and meet legal obligations.

Account creation requirements

  • Display name/username: User-chosen identifier (not required to be real name)
  • Email address: For account verification and essential communications only
  • Password: Minimum 12 characters with complexity requirements; optional MFA enforcement
  • Country/region: General location for content localisation (not a specific address)

Legal compliance data

  • Age verification: Confirmation of minimum age (18+)
  • Terms acceptance: Explicit agreement to community guidelines and terms of service
  • Privacy policy consent: Clear acknowledgment of data handling practices
🙋

Tier 2: Optional Enhancement Data (User-Controlled)

Optional data users may choose to provide to enrich their profile, with full control over their information. Not linked to platform trust level requirements.

Identity information (optional)

  • Real name: Only if user chooses to share
  • Biographical information: User-controlled profile fields
  • Areas of human rights interest and/or expertise: For platform or content discovery

Engagement preferences

  • Communication frequency: How often to receive updates
  • Content areas of interest: Joined networks, groups, or regional focus
  • Language preferences: For multilingual platform support
🚫

Tier 3: Prohibited Data Collection

Data categories that must never be collected without explicit, justified reasons, protecting users from unnecessary privacy risks.

  • Government identification numbers
  • Precise location data (GPS coordinates)
  • Financial information (except for donations processed separately)
  • Detailed demographic profiling beyond what users voluntarily provide
  • Biometric data
  • Data about political opinions beyond human rights advocacy

Next steps

  1. Review recommendations with legal, technical, and campaigning teams
  2. Consult the community on proposed profile collection practices before implementation
  3. Optional community survey for demographics and diversity data collection

Appendix: Age verification providers

To comply with the UK Online Safety Act's age verification requirements in a privacy-preserving manner, AIUK can leverage Zero Knowledge Proof (ZKP) age verification providers. These services allow users to cryptographically prove they are over 18 without sharing personal data.

Age verification can be implemented via an external verification gateway that handles the sensitive verification process independently and then integrates with Discourse through Single Sign-On (SSO).

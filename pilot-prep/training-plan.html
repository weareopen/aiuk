Training
Plan

A scalable training and onboarding plan for AIUK activists and educators — covering platform testing, information sessions, hands-on training content, and onboarding resources for the Community Platform pilot.

About this document

This document outlines the plans and content for onboarding and training for the Community Platform project. The structure of the pilot training set-up and content is designed with the future launch in mind. Training content will be tested and iterated to create evergreen training content for the public launch.

The plan covers three broad phases: platform testing on the staging server, information and training sessions on the production server, and the pilot itself — running in four blocks between February and April 2026.

This document is held in Google Docs and is accessible to project team members.

Introduction

We intend to use the live Community platform for the pre-pilot training so that participants can experience how the platform works. Whilst users will be able to see the entire platform, we will advise them that training activities take place inside the Onboarding and Training category. We'll let them know that they are, of course, welcome to comment on other things they see within the platform, but should be aware that we are actively building the plane they are flying in.

Using the live platform for the training also allows us to save some actions, such as profile creation. We will create a training user group associated with the Onboarding and Training category, so that we can control permissions in the same way we are controlling them in the pilot.

We will also need to advise trainees that we will restrict access after the training and over the holidays, as we likely won't have moderation capabilities at that time.

Finally, we will train community leaders and staff together to develop camaraderie with and between future leaders in the platform.

Platform testing sessions

We will recruit five community members to perform user testing in January. These should be a cross-section of responsibilities and demographics. The aim is to catch any confusion around onboarding and navigation.

As described in our original User Research and Testing guide, we will organise testing around 'missions' — realistic scenarios and tasks that reflect the everyday activities of AIUK community members.

Information and training sessions (TL0/1)

These sessions at the start of each pilot phase will serve as both an information session and a hands-on training. They will cover the aims of the AIUK Community Platform, research carried out, an overview of the Discourse platform, and details of the pilot.

Information component

Purpose

The AIUK Community Platform provides a dedicated online space for activists to connect, collaborate on campaigns, access resources, and drive human rights impact through grassroots organising.

Audience

The platform is intended for AIUK community members, which include activists, educators, and staff members. The initial launch will focus on adults above the age of 18, with the intention of including teen activists. This depends on the successful launch and adoption of the platform, as well as working with legal around the ramifications of the UK Online Safety Act.

Research

Over the last few months, we have carried out research, discovered what our community wants and needs, and performed technical platform reviews. We have now selected an open-source platform called Discourse to become the home of our community. Early next year, we will run a pilot on this platform, and we are glad that you have agreed to participate.

Structure of the pilot

  • Platform testing — Intended to ensure that the initial set-up for the information settings makes sense to a cross-section of community members (November 2025).
  • Information and training sessions — Online meetings for community leaders and staff to inform them of the capabilities of the platform, and to give them hands-on experience of the platform's functionality (January/February 2026).
  • Pilot phases — Mission-based opportunities to explore the platform and give feedback on features, functionality, and set-up through feedback forms and workshops (February–April 2026).

Location

The sessions will take place online using AIUK's Microsoft Teams infrastructure. We will record sessions and also pre-record instructional videos to be embedded on the community platform itself.

Training component

The session will take users through the common functions and features of the community platform that are available to everyone. It will be a hands-on training that gives brief overviews and then asks users to try things for themselves. Participants will be guided to find more complex help on functionality within Discourse documentation.

Content outline — Interface

  • Basic terminology (Topics vs Posts, Categories vs Tags)
  • Topic creation and posting
    • Basic formatting and emoji support
    • Creating polls and rich content
    • Rich media embedding (links auto-expand for YouTube, Wikipedia, etc.)
    • Replying
      • Understanding @mentions and notifications
    • Likes
  • Search (basic and advanced search techniques and filters)
  • Accessibility
    • Dark mode
    • Keyboard navigation
    • Screen reader compatibility

Content outline — Me as user

  • Onboarding
  • Profile
    • Email preferences and digest settings
    • Privacy settings basics
    • Customising notification levels per category and tag
    • Using the activity summary and notification bell

Content outline — Dealing with others

  • Muting
  • Moderation (how it works)
    • Basic flagging system

Admin training session

⚠ Important note on admin access

We highly recommend not giving administrator access to many people. Administrators have the ability to see (and impersonate) users and their personal information, as well as the ability to set all settings on the platform. This power can be massively destructive, as an Admin can essentially break the site for users. We recommend training new admins on a staging server.

There is an open question about whether bespoke admin training should be produced in-house. One view is that we should direct admins to the Discourse training series produced by Jono Bacon — a thorough multi-part guide covering all admin capabilities:

  • Getting Started with Discourse — Part 1
  • Getting Started with Discourse — Part 2
  • Getting Started with Discourse — Part 3

An alternative view is that we should also go through the most commonly-used tools in a live session alongside these resources.

The admin training session will cover:

Interface — Admins

  • Dashboard
  • Plugin management

Me as user — Admins

  • Backups
  • Upgrades
  • Site maintenance
  • Understanding community health metrics

Dealing with others — Admins

  • Site and security settings
  • Categories and permissions
  • Moderate direct messages
  • Manage user groups
  • Impersonate users (troubleshooting/support)
  • Delete/anonymise user accounts
  • Badge system management and custom badges
  • Anonymous mode configuration and oversight
    • Understanding when admins can trace anonymous posts

Onboarding content

Content for the training sessions should live within the Onboarding and Training category on the community platform. Within that category, we envision four subcategories:

  • All the basics — open subcategory
  • Going further — semi-private subcategory, only TL2+ can view
  • Advanced — semi-private subcategory, only TL4, Category Moderators and above can view
  • Site-wide Moderators and Admins

We are creating bespoke training sessions for the AIUK community and content for them. However, we also intend to reference documentation and how-to materials created by the meta Discourse community. This is important for longevity, as the AIUK community Discourse install will upgrade as the meta community develops.

Welcome banner content (site-wide)

The welcome banner in Discourse is a customisable banner that allows the user to dismiss it at any time. This banner is shown by default to new users and can be modified through the "Welcome Banner" topic in the Staff category.

Pinned topics — Onboarding & Training category

When joining the online community, every member has the opportunity to go through an induction both to the platform and to community initiatives run by the AIUK team and other community leaders. In the Onboarding & Training category, which is prominently displayed when users arrive at the platform, community members can access the following pinned content.

Pinned topic: Get Started in the AIUK Community!

Content to be created for this post:

  • Induction video
    • The induction should cover the basics of how to use the online community and what to expect — highlighting things such as the code of conduct, moderation, and how to join groups.
    • This should be in a video no longer than 3 minutes long, which also has options for transcript, BSL signing and subtitles, as well as using simple, accessible language.
    • The induction video should not cover what Amnesty UK is or the structure of the charity. Instead, this information should be signposted in the induction, so members know where to go to find out more.
  • Explore your profile
    • Click on the person icon in the top right corner of the screen and select the "Profile" button on the side menu, then select "Preferences". Here you'll be able to add a profile picture, tell us where in the world you're based, add information about yourself, and modify notification settings.
    • Members to be encouraged to list their interests and expertise, filling out their profile to help them figure out what groups and forum pages to sign up to.
  • Say hello!
    • Members should then be given an opportunity to book in a 1:1 with the community manager or a community organiser, OR book in a monthly/weekly induction training call with the community manager. (Needs to be fleshed out.)
    • Members to be encouraged to list their interests and expertise, taking part in the interest quiz (to be created) to help them figure out what groups and forum pages to sign up to.
    • Members to be encouraged to make a welcome post on a welcome space answering prompt questions such as: What motivates you? What issues matter most to you?
    • Each thematic network (and possibly local group) should have its own induction video on its group page explaining the objectives and set-up of the group.

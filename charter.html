Project Overview

The AIUK Community Platform will provide a dedicated online space for activists to connect, collaborate on campaigns, access resources, and drive human rights impact through grassroots organising. This project involves several phases including planning, research, analysis and strategy design.

What are we building?

A community platform that gives Amnesty International UK activists a home — somewhere to organise, share knowledge, and amplify human rights work together. The project spans discovery, community consultation, technology selection, and a live pilot.

Collaborators & Stakeholders

AIUK is collaborating with We Are Open Co-op (WAO) to deliver this project. The AIUK team responsible is the DDaT team. The project sponsor is Sotez Chowdhury, Director of Activism and Education.

Project Timeline

The project is structured around two-week heartbeats. There is an overview in the AIUK: Project Timeline + Risks document. The team commits to lean processes — saving time and budget for the actual work — while remaining adaptable each heartbeat.

What's a heartbeat?

At the start of each two-week cycle, the project team assesses upcoming tasks. Team members do text-based stand-ups, and at the end of each heartbeat there's a quick retrospective on the previous two weeks.

"It implies a steady, healthy cadence or rhythm — as opposed to endless 'sprinting' or panting against a series of arbitrary deadlines." — Matt Thompson

Project Management

The team uses Trello for project management. Tasks, progress updates, comments and relevant documents are tracked there — preferred over email threads because it associates nuanced tasks with nuanced comments.

Why Trello over email?

There is enough email in the world. Trello lets the team associate nuanced tasks with nuanced comments and keeps a transparent, living record of the project's progress that anyone can check at any time.

Meetings & Communication

Meeting cadence

  • Weekly WAO co-working sessions — collaborative work within the heartbeat
  • Bi-weekly WAO/AIUK check-ins — remove blockers, make decisions, co-work (not a status update)
  • Monthly Community Platform Project Group call — creative advisory group bringing diverse perspectives for decision-makers

For quick questions and coordination, the team uses two Teams messaging channels — one between WAO and Amnesty, and another that includes Torchbox, the agency working on the website and knowledge hub.

External engagement

Feedback loops
  • Mailing lists
  • Personal outreach

The project team commits to working openly by default so that community members can understand the project from day one. Documents without personal information can be viewed by anyone.

Decision Making

Decisions are made collaboratively in biweekly meetings and as notes on Trello cards. The team strives to be transparent so others can understand why decisions were made.

How decisions get made

  • Collaboratively in biweekly meetings and via Trello card notes
  • Acceptance Criteria negotiated amongst the team
  • The Community Platform Project Group models consent-based decision making
  • If a decision can't be reached as a group, the AIUK project sponsor has final say

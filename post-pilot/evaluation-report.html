AIUK Final
Evaluation Report

The summative evaluation of the full project — assessing what was achieved against the original goals, what the pilot revealed, and what comes next for the AIUK activist community platform.

About this report

The AIUK Final Evaluation Report is the second anchor document of the AIUK Community Platform project. It was produced at the conclusion of the pilot phase and provides a summative assessment of the full eighteen-month project — from the original goals set out in the Statement of Work through to the outcomes observed during the pilot.

Where the Technical Discovery Report and Recommendations set out what to build and why, this evaluation report answers the question: did it work, and what did we learn?

What this report is not

This is an evaluation of the project, not the community. A pilot-phase community is still finding its footing — it would be unfair and inaccurate to evaluate the long-term potential of the activist community based on a short pilot. The report evaluates the process, the decisions made, and the early evidence from the pilot against the goals that were set.

Evaluation approach

The evaluation draws on multiple sources of evidence gathered across the project: the original goals and acceptance criteria from the Statement of Work, community metrics tracked during the pilot, feedback from pilot participants, and the project team's own reflective assessment.

01

Goals & criteria

Original SoW goals and the acceptance criteria agreed with AIUK at the start of the project.

02

Pilot metrics

Platform usage data and community metrics tracked against the KPI framework developed in Phase Four.

03

Participant feedback

Feedback gathered from pilot participants — activists, community managers, and AIUK staff — on their experience of the platform.

04

Team reflection

The WAO and AIUK team's own retrospective assessment of what worked and what they would do differently.

What the report evaluates

The evaluation covers the full scope of the project, not only the pilot. It assesses both the process (how the project was run) and the outcomes (what it produced).

🗺️

Research and discovery

Did the research phase give the project team the understanding it needed? Were the right people consulted? Did the findings hold up in practice?

🧭

Community strategy

Did the personas, user journeys, and community design decisions reflect the reality of how activists used the platform during the pilot?

⚙️

Technology selection

Did the recommended platform perform as expected? What worked well in practice, and what required adjustment or workarounds?

🌱

Pilot outcomes

How did activists engage during the pilot? What does early adoption data tell us about the platform's potential for the wider community?

🤝

Ways of working

How well did the heartbeat approach, the WAO/AIUK collaboration, and the project governance model work across eighteen months?

📐

Lessons learned

What would the team do differently? What advice would they give to another organisation embarking on a similar project?

Documents referenced in this report

Like the Technical Discovery Report, this evaluation references a range of project documents — evidence for the assessments it makes. All documents marked ✅ in the project index are referenced in one or both of the anchor documents.

Key referenced documents

  • Platform testing plan with users (Jan 2026) — the structured testing process carried out ahead of the pilot launch
  • Accessibility Testing — assessment of the platform's accessibility against WCAG standards
  • Post-pilot Readiness Assessment Framework — used as a lens for assessing whether the pilot demonstrated readiness to scale
  • Community Metrics — the KPI framework that provided the measurement basis for the evaluation
  • User Journey — used to assess whether the platform actually supported the journeys designed in Phase Four

Looking forward

The evaluation report is not only a retrospective — it also sets direction for what comes next. Based on what the pilot revealed, the report includes recommendations for the next phase of the platform's life: how to grow participation, what to address in the configuration, and how AIUK might sustain and develop the community over time.

These recommendations connect directly to the post-pilot planning documents — the Integration Roadmap and the Community Management Plan — which translate the evaluation's findings into practical next steps.

Companion document

This report should be read alongside the Technical Discovery Report and Recommendations, which sets out the strategic and technical basis that this evaluation assesses. Together they form the complete record of evidence-based decision-making across the project.

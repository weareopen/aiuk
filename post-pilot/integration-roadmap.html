Post Pilot Planning:
Integration Roadmap

A sequenced plan for the integrations and platform developments planned for after the Q1 2026 pilot — from search and SSO through to CRM and events.

About this document

This document serves as the reference point for community platform developments and integrations planned for the post-pilot phase. Many features identified in the Technical Discovery Report were prioritised for the Q1 2026 pilot; the items here represent the next wave of enhancements to build out the wider digital ecosystem.

Each item below is assigned an approximate target window and names the teams and technical dependencies involved.

This document is held in Google Docs and is accessible to project team members.

Original on Google Drive (unavailable after May 2026)

Planned integrations

March 2026

Search

Users should be able to search across Amnesty's digital platforms from within the community platform. However, the content of the community platform should not be surfaced via the Knowledge Hub or AIUK website — search should be one-directional.

One technical approach is the Discourse Algolia Search plugin. However, Torchbox is using Elastic Search for the Knowledge Hub and Website, so the preferred approach needs to be agreed with them and with Communiteq before implementation.

April 2026

Website Announcements

Shortly after the pilot, announcements from the community platform should be surfaced on the AIUK website. This would allow community activity to reach a broader audience and drive sign-ups from existing website visitors.

The approach will be explored collaboratively with Torchbox (TBX) and Communiteq in the months following the pilot.

May 2026

Single Sign-On (SSO)

The Technical Discovery Report recommended that Single Sign-On be implemented from the public launch phase and, if possible, during the pilot. SSO depends on collaboration across the Website, Knowledge Hub, and IT teams within the wider digital transformation project.

Technically, SSO is not complex — the platform was selected in part for its SSO capability. However, as of autumn 2025, other teams had not yet identified a need for SSO. The current plan is to provide SSO for staff members in the short term, with expansion to other community members as the CRM and Supporter Portal projects mature.

Dependency note

SSO implementation across AIUK's digital ecosystem will require agreement from the Website, Knowledge Hub, and IT teams. Progress is contingent on those projects advancing.

Late 2026

CRM (Salesforce)

AIUK selected Salesforce as its new CRM, to be implemented throughout 2026 and into 2027. As part of that project, careful consideration needs to be given to what data is synchronised between the community platform and the CRM.

One potential integration to explore: enabling users to associate pseudonyms with real names in a secure way, so that they can update supporter information directly from the community platform.

Data flows across the ecosystem will require dedicated data privacy planning and adherence to the principles established in the platform's data protection documentation.

Late 2026

Events

When the Community Manager joined in summer 2025, one of the first things she identified was the variety of event management tools in use across the organisation. Since then, the team has begun exploring calendar and events functionality via a plugin directly on the community platform.

Full research into event requirements has not yet been completed. The recommendation is to return to this area after the pilot and public launch, when real usage patterns will inform what functionality is genuinely needed.

Context and approach

The integrations in this roadmap were deliberately deferred from the pilot phase to keep the initial launch focused. The pilot was designed to test core community features — discussion, networking, profiles, and moderation — without the complexity that integrations introduce.

Working across the digital transformation project

Several integrations (Search, SSO, CRM) depend on decisions and progress from other workstreams within AIUK's wider digital transformation. The community platform team will need to stay aligned with the Website, Knowledge Hub, and IT teams as those projects evolve.

Data privacy considerations

Each integration — particularly CRM synchronisation — carries data privacy implications. All integrations should be assessed against the Data Privacy for the Community Platform documentation before implementation begins.

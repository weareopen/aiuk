Considerations for 55+ Demographic

An accessibility-focused comparison of the four shortlisted platforms for AIUK's community — examining how each serves activists aged 55 and over across usability, accessibility features, and organisational tools.

About this document

For those aged 55 and over, accessibility is a significant factor when selecting a communication platform. All the platforms shortlisted (Mattermost, Rocket.Chat, Zulip, and Discourse) make accessibility a priority, but there are meaningful differences in how well each serves this demographic.

This document assesses the four shortlisted platforms specifically for the needs of AIUK's 55+ activist community, covering visual accessibility options, ease of use, organisational features, and onboarding support.

This document is held in Google Docs and is accessible to project team members.

Key factors for the 55+ demographic

Accessibility and confidence

For activists aged 55 and over, three factors make the biggest difference to successful participation: visual accessibility options, ease of use that builds confidence, and strong onboarding support. Platforms that score well on all three are most likely to achieve genuine engagement from this demographic.

  • Accessibility: Adjustable text size, high-contrast modes, and keyboard navigation are particularly helpful for users with visual or dexterity needs.
  • Ease of use: Confidence and willingness to participate are strongly influenced by how intuitive a platform feels. Familiar interface patterns reduce barriers to engagement.
  • Onboarding support: Clear instructions and help resources are valuable for those less familiar with new technology.
  • Communication format: Some users in this demographic prefer slower-paced, reflective discussion rather than real-time chat.

Platform highlights for 55+

Recommended for accessibility

Rocket.Chat

Stands out for users with visual or dexterity needs, with BITV 2.0 (Germany) and WCAG 2.1 AA certification. Offers high-contrast modes, adjustable font sizes, and strong keyboard navigation. Highly customisable appearance with simple interface.

Familiar interface

Mattermost

Offers a familiar chat layout that is straightforward for anyone accustomed to standard messaging services. Intuitive navigation and clear notifications. Strong on accessibility, though may require onboarding support for less tech-savvy users.

Good with guidance

Zulip

Topic-based threading keeps conversations organised, though some users may need initial guidance. Excellent onboarding resources and help centre. Configurable font size, line spacing, and high-contrast options. Strong for structured conversation.

Best for reflective discussion

Discourse

The forum format will be recognisable to many who have used message boards in the past. Well suited to slower-paced, considered discussion rather than instant replies. Supports large fonts, high contrast, and keyboard navigation.

Recommendations for this demographic

Rocket.Chat: best for accessibility compliance

For AIUK activists with visual impairments or dexterity needs, Rocket.Chat's industry-leading WCAG 2.1 AA certification and BITV 2.0 compliance make it the strongest option. Its highly customisable appearance allows users to adapt the interface to their needs.

Discourse: best for familiar, considered discussion

For activists in this age group who are comfortable with forum-style interaction and prefer thoughtful, asynchronous discussion over instant messaging, Discourse provides a familiar and well-structured environment.

Onboarding is key

Whichever platform is chosen, investing in clear onboarding materials — particularly for users less familiar with digital tools — will be critical to achieving genuine engagement from the 55+ demographic. Zulip and Rocket.Chat both provide strong onboarding resources out of the box.

For real-time group conversations, Mattermost, Rocket.Chat, and Zulip are all suitable choices. Discourse, by comparison, is well suited to communities that prefer more reflective, considered discussion rather than instant replies.

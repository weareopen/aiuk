Processes and Workflows

The social and cultural processes needed to make the AIUK Community Platform flourish — covering moderation, documentation, coordination, onboarding, and the responsibilities of each team across the organisation.

About this document

A community is more than its platform. Outside of the functional requirements for the new AIUK community platform, there are a variety of social and cultural processes that need to be established to ensure the community develops and flourishes. This document records those processes in relation to the different platforms in the ecosystem and the teams that manage those tools.

Coordination between teams at AIUK is essential to support the community and its individual members.

The core challenge

"Moving from siloed activism/events, partnerships, HRE participation and lopsided activism activity to a much more interconnected activist and learner base with supported journeys where they can flow through and benefit from our expertise and resources."

— Sotez Chowdhury

Overarching leadership activities

Regardless of team or function, certain activities must be owned at a leadership level to ensure consistent direction across the entire community platform initiative:

  • Document community pathways that help visualise how community members deepen their engagement and build trust within both the system and the community
  • Develop a community training and engagement programme as well as staff support for the platform stack
  • Assign engagement and moderation responsibilities amongst AIUK staff
  • Implement training for staff, activists and educators around the community strategy, moderation practices, features, and tools

Activism & Education Team

The ownership challenge

"Many staff reported ambiguity about who owns what, especially in relation to digital content, delivery, and user journeys."

— TBX Stakeholder Interviews

People manage and shape a community, so the Activism & Education team should work together to ensure that AIUK's programming and campaigns are visible to the community. The team should encourage open participation — meaning that everyone in the community can contribute.

As staff members work together with community members, they should pay particular attention to balancing the needs of long-standing activists (likely in the 55+ age group) with the needs of new or episodic activists. Staff should engage closely with moderation volunteers and co-design processes and resources together with them.

Community Platform: Alignment
Moderation, documentation & coordination

Moderation

  • Sufficient moderation and staff pinning or highlighting public community content
  • Staff and community oversight and a process for "pulling up" interesting themes and content to the community platform landing page
  • Community mentorship programming — documenting structures that help long-term AIUK activists mentor newer community members
  • Moderation and leadership training for community organisers

Documentation

  • Update the Community Rules to be clearer around what constitutes misinformation and anti-inclusive behaviour or language
  • Document moderation and posting processes
  • Clear trust progression pathways — community members need to understand how and when they receive access or abilities; such processes should be co-designed
  • Group/Network procedures — clear onboarding, visuals and descriptions of how public and private spaces work and who can access them

Coordination and Structuring

  • Establish a weekly and monthly cadence as well as an open community calendar
  • Community cadence — community guidance around how and when different sub-communities interact with their members
  • Feedback rounds — ensuring that community members have an opportunity to provide feedback at every step, especially around their own visibility

DDaT Team

The Digital Transformation team plays a central role in not only getting the Community Platform off the ground, but in advocating for internal processes and practices that will help make it a success. As the team implements various platforms, they should be aware of things that directly impact the community.

🌐

Website: Invitations

Detail trust learning pathways, give limited CMS permissions to community members, create group/network overview page templates, and establish a process for showcasing activist actions.

📚

Knowledge Hub: Learning

Document community rules and workflows. Provide organised onboarding and best practice content for grassroots organising, plus how-to content for the community platform stack.

💬

Chat Apps: Coordination

Provide templates and best practices for chat apps in the Knowledge Hub. Support community organisers in modelling good behaviour and establishing a "phone book" of group/network links.

Website: Invitations
Processes for community presence on the AIUK website
  • Detail trust learning pathways to establish progression procedures for gaining CMS permissions
  • Establish giving limited permissions to community members to update group/network overview pages
  • Create a Group/Network overview page template prompting content such as a link to the community platform, social media handles, and chat channels
  • A staff process to decide what activist actions (e.g. community-created petitions or events) to showcase
Knowledge Hub: Learning content
Starter and ongoing content requirements
  • Starter content for everything currently being decided
  • Permissions for community organisers to work within the community documentation section of the Knowledge Hub
  • Submission workflow for community activists
Chat Apps: Coordination
Supporting decentralised community communication
  • Templates and best practices in the Knowledge Hub for chat apps for groups created by activists without AIUK staff oversight
  • Community organisers model behaviour to use best practices and recommended apps with specific outreach and engagement
  • Community organisers help establish a "phone book" by making sure group/network overview pages have links to chats

Campaigns & Communications Team

The visibility problem

"Local groups feel disconnected from national campaigns and from each other. Their activities and successes are often invisible: the website doesn't convey the level of activity and dynamism within the activist network. Activism and fundraising efforts by local groups and networks may not be well-integrated with central campaigns, missing opportunities to build a bigger picture of Amnesty's work and impact."

— TBX Stakeholder Interviews

The Campaigns and Communications team plays an important role in celebrating the existence and grassroots activism of individuals, groups and networks within the AIUK community. In coordination with the Activism & Events team, this team should work to integrate engagement programmes and communication initiatives with the community platform.

Social Media: Broadcast
Amplifying community activity externally
  • Broadcasting milestones that should be celebrated
  • Creating a submission workflow from community organisers to the communications team
  • Making sure campaign round-ups include insights from community organisers
  • Engaging with community organisers on calls to action and staying in dialogue with groups and networks

Onboarding, training and engagement

The AIUK Community Manager provided learning acquired from benchmarking other activist communities. The following summarises the engagement-focused insights across three areas.

Onboarding
First impressions and first steps
  • Welcome Videos and Guides – Displayed when first joining, with subtitles and an alternative text introduction for those who do not wish to view video
  • Live Community induction – Offered to members when first signing up; chance for them to speak to real people for more information or to answer questions
  • Community guidelines – Guidelines visible when first signing up and pinned to a page where posting is available
  • Tips for posting – Tips given before members post, such as what wording gets the most responses, plus a link to community guidelines
  • Moderation Guides – Clear procedure of when content will be moderated, what to do to submit a report, and what happens if guidelines are infringed
Training
Building capability across the community
  • Training space – A place to see volunteering roles and vacancies or to access training
  • Platform training programme – Asynchronous training materials to help users get the most out of the Community Platform
Engagement
Sustaining participation over time
  • Engagement badges – Recognition for participation, such as 'liked 100 times' badges or 'streak features' showing how engaged members have been
  • Personalised messages – A personalised approach to entice more members to groups and satisfy aims around automatic onboarding and personalised practices
  • Ask an expert – A space to speak to staff and community leaders; results can be anonymised and kept on the platform as an FAQ
